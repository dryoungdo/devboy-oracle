**Key insights**:

1. **Trust is the bottom of the stack**. You can have perfect roles + RACI matrices and still fail without trust. Lencioni's 5 Dysfunctions stack from "absence of trust" upward.

2. **Trust is dimension-specific.** Don't say "I don't trust X" — say "I don't trust X's [reliability / sincerity / competence / care]." The fix is dimension-specific (Feltman).

3. **Trust gains slowly, loses fast.** Asymmetric. One betrayal can wipe months of building. Repair requires loud accountability + visible behavior change.

4. **Role clarity is the easiest leverage point.** Most "trust issues" are actually role ambiguity. Define boundaries, interfaces, escalation paths. Half the trust friction disappears.

5. **Role drift is the silent killer.** Doing others' work without explicit hand-off (today: my tmux send-keys to MLBOY's pane) violates Boundaries (BRAVING-B). Even good intent costs trust.

6. **2-pizza ceiling is real.** DO fleet at 9 BOYs + Captain + P'Nat is at the limit. Coordination via maw/ψ/inbox/retros must be load-bearing or chaos.

7. **Single-sentence**: Trust is the medium; Role is the structure; Responsibility is the duty; Accountability is the singular ownership; Teamwork is what emerges when all four are clear, mutual, and reliable.
