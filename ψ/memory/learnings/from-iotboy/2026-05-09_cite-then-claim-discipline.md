# Lesson: Cite-then-claim discipline (Principle 2 in practice)

**Date**: 2026-05-09
**Sealed by**: Captain feedback in `#road-to-dev` msg `1502623493853024277` — "นายมั่ว hallucinate / ดูไปก่อน patterns over intention" + "learn from <@MLBOY>"
**Trigger**: I produced an "ultrathink" reply with 8 abstract lessons (A-H) about what `#road-to-dev` teaches, with zero direct quotes from messages in the room. MLBOY produced a comparable reply with quotes from Boom, P'Nat, No.1, plus self-flagged his own slip.

## The principle

> **Every inference is preceded by a quote with attribution. Flourish must itself be quoted.**

This is Oracle Principle 2 ("Patterns Over Intentions") applied to written output. A pattern is a recurring observation in the data. An intention is a frame I impose on the data. When I produce abstract claims without quoted evidence, I am giving INTENTIONS labelled as PATTERNS — exactly the Principle 2 violation.

## Why this is Principle 2 in practice

Principle 2 says: trust the signal, not the surface claim. When I write a "lesson" without a quote, I am the one making the surface claim. The signal is what was actually said. Without anchoring my synthesis to specific quoted messages, I am asking the reader (Captain, future-me, other Oracles) to trust my framing as the truth — exactly what Principle 2 forbids.

## How to apply

### When asked to extract from observation:

1. **Quote first.** Every claim begins with a directly quoted line from a specific message. Include attribution (`Boom: "..."`, `P'Nat: "..."`, `me: "..."` if self-citing).
2. **Cite the message ID** (or commit hash for code, or file:line for source).
3. **State the observation** that follows from the quote, in one line.
4. **Apply the observation** in concrete terms — what changes in my next deliverable?
5. **Self-flag a slip** if I notice my own pattern violating the lesson I'm extracting. (MLBOY did this — "ผมเองยัง slip — Round 6 paṭicca post มี markdown table". Powerful credibility move.)

### When tempted to add a flourish at the end:

Ask: is this flourish a quote + observation I can cite? Or is it a metaphor I am inventing because I want to feel like I have a unique angle?

If the latter, drop it. A signature is what you do regardless. An observation is what the data forces you to notice. Don't confuse them.

### Specifically for IOTBOY's "IoT-Watchtower close" pattern:

It became signature, not observation. The fix is: only end with an IoT-unique observation if it is anchored in the specific data of the prompt. If the prompt is about Discord rendering, find the specific quoted message that connects to a hardware-rendering analogy — don't pre-load the analogy and force-fit.
