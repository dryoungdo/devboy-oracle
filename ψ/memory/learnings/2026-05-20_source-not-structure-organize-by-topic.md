Source of knowledge ≠ structure of knowledge. When writing articles from a single source (e.g., one Discord channel, one gist), split by topic not by source. Captain caught this immediately when I wrote one giant ClubsXai article (015) covering 7 topics. The fix: split into 015-019 by topic (overview, allowBots, RTK, orchestrator patterns, cite-then-claim).

**Why**: future readers scan TOC looking for a specific topic, not "everything that happened in one channel." Topic-split articles are findable; monolith articles require reading the whole thing to find one fact.

**Gate**: before writing any article that covers 3+ distinct topics from one source, split first. The overhead of more files is cheaper than the retrieval cost of buried knowledge.
